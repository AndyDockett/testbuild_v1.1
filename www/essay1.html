We measure the world away By Morten svenstrup It’s utterly impossible to imagine how our world would look without numbers. I wonder if numbers actually change our relationship with our environment — the things around us, the world we live in. We understand numbers as something very concrete. But numbers might be the most abstract thing there is. Numbers are not present in nature, they represent an exclusively human way of thinking. Numbers need a context to have any meaning. If, for example, I don’t say ‘five planets’ but only ‘five’, practically nothing has been said...
